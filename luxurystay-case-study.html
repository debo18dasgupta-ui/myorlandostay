01 The Story

Booking a stay should feel like entering the experience before arriving.

LuxuryStay explores how a travel booking website can move beyond basic listings and filters to create a more emotional, visual, and premium discovery experience.

02 The Problem

Most hotel booking websites feel functional but emotionally flat.

They help users compare rooms, but they rarely help users imagine the mood, comfort, and lifestyle of the stay.

03 The Process

Mapping the experience before styling it

I started with rough notes and low-fidelity wireframes to understand the page hierarchy, booking flow, and storytelling rhythm.

04 Wireframes

05 Visual Exploration

Finding the right mood

I explored different visual directions — from lighter hospitality layouts to darker, warmer, more cinematic compositions. The final direction leaned into deep browns, warm gold, and large imagery to feel more premium.

07 Reflection

What I learned

Less is more.

Luxury UX is not about adding more details. It is about creating the right pacing, restraint, and emotional atmosphere.

Visuals lead the emotion.

The right imagery, color, and typography help users imagine the experience before they even book it.

Design with intention.

Every section was designed with a purpose — to inform, to build trust, or to inspire exploration.

This project strengthened my belief that good design doesn't just help users book a stay, it helps them feel it.
